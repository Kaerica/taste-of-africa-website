Kelewele Ghana

West Africa
About This Dish

Kelewele is Ghana's most beloved street food — fried ripe plantains marinated in a punchy spice mix of ginger, cayenne pepper, and ground cloves. The result: sweet caramelised plantain with a fiery, fragrant crust that is completely addictive.

Sold from roadside stalls all over Accra every evening, kelewele is eaten as a snack or side. The key is very ripe plantains — almost black-skinned — where the natural sugar creates that unmistakable golden caramelisation.

▶ Ingredients (Expandable Details Section) ▾
  • 3 very ripe plantains (mostly black skin)
  • 2 tsp fresh ginger, grated (or 1 tsp ground)
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper
  • ¼ tsp ground cloves
  • ½ tsp salt
  • Optional: pinch of anise or grains of selim (hwentia)
  • Vegetable oil for deep frying
Cooking Instructions
1
Peel plantains and cut into 2cm chunks or diagonal slices. Place in a bowl.
2
Mix ginger, cayenne, cloves, and salt. Pour over plantain pieces and toss to coat every surface. Marinate 15–30 minutes for deeper flavour.
3
Heat vegetable oil in a deep pan to 175°C (350°F). Fry in small batches for 3–4 minutes turning once, until deep golden-brown and caramelised. Never overcrowd the pan.
4
Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Serve immediately while hot and crispy. Traditionally served with roasted peanuts or alongside beans stew.
