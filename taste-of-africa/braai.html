South African Braai South Africa

Southern Africa
About This Dish

The braai is South Africa's most powerful cultural institution — far more than a barbecue. Around a fire, South Africans of every background gather. The centrepiece is always boerewors — farmer's sausage made from beef, pork, and coriander seed, coiled onto the grid.

Alongside sit lamb chops, sosaties (marinated skewers with apricot jam and curry), and pap (stiff maize porridge). Heritage Day is unofficially National Braai Day — a fire-lit celebration of South African identity.

▶ Ingredients (Expandable Details Section) ▾
  • 1kg boerewors sausage, coiled
  • 6 lamb loin chops
  • 500g boneless lamb for sosaties
  • Sosatie marinade: apricot jam, curry powder, wine vinegar
  • Braai spice: coriander, smoked paprika, garlic salt, black pepper
  • 2 cups maize meal for pap
  • Chakalaka (spiced bean relish) to serve
  • Hardwood for the fire — never gas
Cooking Instructions
1
Light hardwood in your braai. Allow 45–60 minutes to burn down to a solid bed of glowing coals. Hold your hand 15cm above the grid — it should be very hot but tolerable for 3 seconds.
2
Thread sosatie cubes onto skewers with dried apricots and onion. Marinate overnight in apricot jam, curry powder, grated onion, and wine vinegar.
3
Place boerewors coils on the grid — never pierce them. Grill 8–10 min per side. Season lamb chops with braai spice and grill 4–5 min per side. Rotate sosaties every 3 minutes.
4
Cook pap by stirring maize meal into boiling salted water for 20 minutes. Serve all meats from the grid with pap, chakalaka, and braai bread grilled over the coals.
