Isombe Rwanda

East Africa
About This Dish

Isombe is one of Rwanda's most cherished national dishes — pounded cassava leaves cooked with eggplant and thickened with groundnut paste. Silky, earthy, and deeply nourishing.

Traditionally served alongside ugali or boiled sweet potato. The addition of dried smoked fish is common in households near Lake Kivu, adding a layer of depth that transforms the dish entirely.

▶ Ingredients (Expandable Details Section) ▾
  • 500g fresh or frozen cassava leaves
  • 1 medium eggplant, cubed
  • 3 tbsp groundnut paste (peanut butter)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp palm oil or vegetable oil
  • 1 cup vegetable stock
  • Optional: 50g dried smoked fish
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
Cooking Instructions
1
Wash cassava leaves, strip from stems, and pound or blend coarsely. If frozen, thaw and drain well.
2
Heat oil in a large pot. Fry onions until golden (7 min), add garlic for 1 minute, then add eggplant and stir 3 minutes.
3
Add cassava leaves and stock. Bring to a boil then simmer covered for 25–30 minutes, stirring occasionally until leaves are tender.
4
Stir in groundnut paste, cook uncovered 5 more minutes until thick and rich. Season well. Serve hot with ugali or sweet potato.
