Somali Suqaar Somalia

Horn of Africa
About This Dish

Suqaar is one of Somalia's most widely eaten everyday dishes — a fast, satisfying sauté of diced meat seasoned with Somalia's signature spice blend: xawaash. Xawaash is the soul of Somali cooking — a warm blend of cumin, coriander, turmeric, black pepper, cardamom, and cinnamon.

Served for breakfast or lunch alongside canjeero (spongy fermented flatbread) or bariis iskukaris (Somali spiced rice). The key is high heat and a good sear — the crust on each cube of meat makes all the difference.

▶ Ingredients (Expandable Details Section) ▾
  • 500g beef, goat, or chicken — 1cm cubes
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 2 green peppers, diced
  • 2 large tomatoes, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • Xawaash: 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp coriander, ½ tsp turmeric, ½ tsp black pepper, ¼ tsp cardamom, pinch cinnamon
  • 3 tbsp vegetable oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander, salt to taste
Cooking Instructions
1
Mix the xawaash spice blend. Heat oil in a large pan over high heat until smoking hot. Add meat in a single layer and sear undisturbed 2–3 minutes until a brown crust forms.
2
Remove seared meat and set aside. In the same pan over medium heat, fry onions for 5–6 minutes until golden. Add garlic for 1 minute.
3
Add xawaash spice blend, stir constantly for 1 minute to bloom. Add tomatoes and green peppers, cook 5–7 minutes until softened and saucy.
4
Return seared meat to pan. Cook together 3–5 minutes until done through. Season generously with salt. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve immediately.
